about CHIRP:

CHIRP (Chicago Independent Radio Project) is a volunteer-driven, listener supported community radio station formed in 2007 in Chicago, IL. They can be heard everyday on 107.1FM in the city or online at CHIRPRadio.org

refreshing their app:

Originally created in 2013, the CHIRP Radio app is due for a refresh, with an update to the most recent guidelines set out by Android & Apple's operating systems.

did you know?

When designing a native app, it’s best to stick as closely as possible to each platform's design guide. Following specified UI guidelines helps ensure that users of either mobile platform can intuitively start using the app right away.

project brief:

Design two versions of a CHIRP native mobile app for Android (Material 3) & Apple (iOS 17):
Native mobile design patterns using each platform’s guidelines
Clearly defined typography & color
Consistent, intuitive UI design
User gestures demonstrated
Appropriate use of UI feedback
Designed for accessibility

key functions & user flow:

The most important function of the app is to play the latest song streaming live on CHIRP Radio. Based on research of other community radio apps. a few other key functions were also defined but kept to a minimum:
1. Sign In or Create Account
2. Listen to What’s Playing Now
3. Request a Song
4. Save or Share Fav Songs
5. Read DJ Track Notes
6. Listen to Past Shows
7. Adjust Account/App Settings
8. View Events & Shop Merch
9. Donate to CHIRP

typography:

color contrast accessibility:

Color contrast refers to the difference in light between font (or anything in the foreground) and its background. Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) require a certain ratio of color contrast for people with vision disabilities. The goal is to meet at least WCAG Level AA conformance.

colors:

Same colors are used on both designs, starting with Apple colors then creating tints & shades. (Apple’s operating system has specific recommended colors while Android does not.)

logo refresh:

CHIRP bird logo was cleaned up and simplified for an app icon using Adobe Illustrator, and their old album placeholder image was updated in Figma.

icon design:

Volume levels were designed as a silhouette of the Chicago skyline. Icons and interfaces were designed in accordance with each platform’s guidelines.

mid-fidelity wireframes:

Grayscale prototype developed in Figma.

high-fidelity wireframes:

A realistic prototype that closely resembles the final design each platform’s app was created in Figma.
