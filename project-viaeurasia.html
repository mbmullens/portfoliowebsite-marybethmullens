Case Study: ViaEurasia

walking trail app

Redesign of the logo and UI of an app for Via Eurasia (a historic hiking path from Europe to Asia).

view the interactive prototype

Quicklinks: About | Project Brief | Logo | Colors | Wireframes & Prototype | UI Kit Assets |

about Via Eurasia:

The Via Eurasia is a series of long distance hiking trails linking Europe to Asia, using ancient historic routes and paths. The trail system was developed by Turkey’s Culture Routes Society and funded by the European Union.

project brief:

The Culture Routes Society requested a redesign of their intial app to fix many problems based on poor user feedback:
1. Outdated logo and branding
2. Colors not accessible
3. Confusing navigation
4. More screens than necessary
5. Text needs better hierarchy
6. “Home” page looks like legal document
7. “About” page lacks visual appeal

new logo concepts:

Their original logo was a pair of footprints to represent ancient travelers who once walked barefoot on this trail. However, this idea is unclear unless explained, and the old logo lacks aesthetic appeal.

The new logo designs are meant to convey the concept of the trail and app quicker plus refine the overall look.

colors:

Colors chosen were green to emanate the outdoors and purple to represent the grandeur of these historic routes.

They contrast properly when applied with type and backgrounds to meet the WCAG (Web Content Accessibility Guidelines) standards.

high-fidelity wireframes:

A realistic prototype was created in Figma, including many interactive elements.
