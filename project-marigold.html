Case Study: Marigold Clothing

women's boutique branding

Brand Guide Design booklet for Marigold Clothing, highlighting the identity, marketing and website of a successful Indianapolis boutique open since 1989.

visit Marigold's website

Quicklinks: Cover | Brand Identity | Guiding Principles | Logo | Icon | Colors | Typography | Imagery | Website |

cover:

brand identity:

Marigold Clothing's identity is centered around making a genuine connection with their audience. Essential quotes plus photos of staff and customers help emphasize this idea.

guiding principles:

Marigold can best be summed up with 3 core principles: providing Personal Service, Unique Style and Quality Product.

logo:

Marigold's primary logo is a cleaned up and streamlined variation of their original logo from 1989.

icon:

A new bold flower graphic replaced the original "folksy" flower drawing.

colors & accessibility:

Marigold's core brand colors remain orange and lime green, but darker shades were needed to meet WCAG Level AA Standards on light backgrounds.

WCAG (Web Content Accessibility Guidelines) requires a minimum ratio of color contrast between fonts or icons and their background to accomodate people with visual disabilities.

typography:

Brandon Grotesque's sans-serif typeface was chosen for it's contemporary yet enduring appearance.
Buhkari's easy-to-read handwriting font adds extra flair to ads or social media.

imagery & marketing:

Marketing collateral and social media aim for a personal and artistic approach.

website:

Since Marigold features a lot of funky fashion, a balance was sought after on the website with a simple. clean interface and consistent product photography.
