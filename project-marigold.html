Case Study: Marigold Clothing

boutique brand guide

Brand Guideline Design booklet for Marigold Clothing, a successful Indianapolis clothing store open since 1989.

visit Marigold's website

brand identity:

Marigold's taglines are "Making fashion fun since 1989" and "My Style. My Store." Their identity is based on making genuine connections with their customers. Quotes and photos of Marigold staff were shown to emphasize this idea.

guiding principles:

Marigold Clothing's 3 core principles are Personal Service, Unique Style and Quality Product. They focus on helping people find their own individual style with pieces that stand the test of time, rather than fast throwaway fashion.

logo:

Marigold's primary logo is a cleaned up and streamlined variation of their original logo from 1989.

icon:

Their flower icon was re-drawn entirely with more modern vector aesthetics, as the old one was deemed to "folksy."

colors & accessibility:

The core brand colors are orange and lime green, but darker shades of both were added as alternative options for better color contrast on white backgrounds when needed.

typography:

Brandon Grotesque's sans-serif typeface was chosen for it's contemporary yet enduring appearance. Buhkari's bold, easy to read handwriting font adds extra emphasis and flair on ads and social media.

imagery & marketing:

Ads, flyers, social media posts and photography aim to be personal, bold, and eye-catching.

website:

Since Marigold features artistic and funky fashion, a balance was needed with a more simpler, modern website design and clean photography on white backgrounds for consistency.
