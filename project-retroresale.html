Case Study: Retro Resale

vintage resale app

UX/UI design of a native mobile app for buying/selling vintage clothes.

view the interactive prototype

Quicklinks: Project Brief | Competitors | Mood Board | Colors | Typography | Logo & Icons | User Flow | Wireframes |


project brief:

Design the user interface for a vintage clothing app. Target audience is primarily 25 to 34 year olds who want to buy and sell vintage clothing online.

competitor research:

Resale Apps Explored: Vestiaire Collective, ASOS Marketplace, Etsy, Vinted and Poshmark.

Design principles researcheds: Jakob’s Nielsen’s 10 Usability Heuristics for Interaction Design.

mood board:

Words that defined the board were "eclectic, retro, vintage, vibes, playful and stylish." Photos selected represent many eras of fashion from the 1920's to 2000's.

colors:

The colors are a mix of pastels and earth tones, in bold and muted palettes.

typography:

The incentive in picking Century Gothic was finding a font that spanned multiple decades but still looked retro.

logo & icons:

The logo is inspired by mid-century modern graphics. The largest icons were modeled after 90s computer graphics.

user flow chart:

The User Flow was sketched out based on the app's key functionalities:
1. Create a profile
2. Upload items to sell
3. Leave reviews for other sellers
4. Browse product for sale
5. Save items to buy
6. Add items to shopping cart

low fidelity wireframes:

Initial wireframes were sketched out by hand with detail to visualize the project.

high-fidelity wireframes:

A realistic prototype was created in Figma that would closely resembling the final app design.
